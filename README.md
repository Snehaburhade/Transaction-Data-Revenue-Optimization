Project Overview
Data is just noise until it tells a story. In this project, I took a deep dive into customer credit card behavior to uncover what actually drives revenue. By bridging the gap between raw demographics and spending habits, I developed a dual-dashboard system designed to help stakeholders optimize marketing spend, refine card offerings, and boost customer retention.

Before any visualization, I treated the data in Microsoft Excel to ensure total accuracy:

De-duplication: Stripped out redundant records to prevent skewed metrics.

Data Integrity: Identified and filled gaps in the data using logical context-based mapping.

Standardization: Unified currency, dates, and numerical formats to ensure the dashboards reflect "one version of the truth."

üìä Phase 1: Transaction & Revenue Behavior
This stage focused on how the money moves. Key discoveries included:

The "Blue" Dominance: While luxury cards get the hype, the Blue Card is the workhorse, generating over 83% of total revenue ($55M).

Peak Cycles: Activity peaks in Q3, suggesting seasonal spending trends that marketing teams can capitalize on.

Swipe is King: Despite the rise of digital, 63.6% of transactions still happen via physical swipes, highlighting a traditional user base.

Top Triggers: "Bills" and "Entertainment" are the primary drivers of card usage, accounting for nearly 44% of all spending.
